type: world_system

## Overview
Across the Western Continent, most cultures recognize a shared framework of timekeeping known informally as the Common Calendar. While local traditions, festivals, and seasonal observances vary widely, the names and structure of the months are broadly understood for the purposes of trade, record-keeping, and historical reference.

The calendar year consists of **12 months**, each made up of **four weeks** of **seven days**, for a total of **336 days**.

The year is divided evenly into **four seasons**, each lasting **three months**.

## Cultural Notes
- Month names are widely recognized even where local pronunciations or epithets differ.
- Festivals, holy days, and seasonal rites vary greatly by culture, faith, and region.
- Contracts and charters typically reference **month and week**, rather than specific days.
- Years are often remembered by notable events rather than numbered chronologies.

---

## Usage
This calendar provides a shared narrative and administrative framework. Individual regions may attach unique meanings, taboos, or celebrations to each month without altering the underlying structure.
